## Design Context

### Users
Cybersecurity hiring managers and recruiters who land on this portfolio to evaluate Alec Lopez as a SOC Analyst / Blue Team candidate. They are time-constrained, scanning for signals of competence and initiative. The site must communicate credibility within 3 seconds and reward deeper reading with evidence of hands-on technical ability. Secondary audience: peers and mentors in the cybersecurity community.

### Brand Personality
**Resolute. Precise. Grounded.**
Alec's brand is "Blue Team Defender. Relentless by Nature." — someone who builds, ships, and proves. The tone is confident without arrogance, technical without jargon-heavy, and warm without being casual. Think: a craftsman showing their workshop, not a salesman pitching.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Editorial minimalism with warm, light palette. Inspired by April Taylor (generous whitespace, typography-driven hierarchy, restrained color), Conductor (clean information architecture, monospace accents), and Magnet Lover (warm tones, bold accents used sparingly).
- **Theme**: Light mode only. Warm parchment cream (`#FAF7F2`) background, umber brown accent (`#6B4C3B`), shield gold (`#B8943E`) used sparingly.
- **Typography**: Cormorant Garamond for headings (elegant serif with 16th-century roots — the key design motif tying to Alec's medieval/fantasy interests without any literal fantasy imagery). Body in DM Sans (warmer than Inter, pairs well with Cormorant Garamond). IBM Plex Mono for technical labels and tags.
- **Anti-references**: No hacker aesthetic. No dark terminals, green-on-black, matrix effects, circuit boards, or cyber-punk motifs. No generic template look. No purple gradients. No cards nested inside cards.
- **Signature element**: A minimal geometric shield/crest SVG with "AL" initials — reads as a clean personal logo to recruiters, as a personal coat of arms to Alec. Sections use a "chapter numbering" system (01 / ABOUT, 02 / PROJECTS) evoking manuscript structure.

### Design Principles

1. **Substance over spectacle** — Every element earns its place by communicating something. No decoration for its own sake. The home lab projects are the hero, and the design exists to make them shine.

2. **Typography is the design** — With a light, minimal palette, the type system does the heavy lifting. Cormorant Garamond at display sizes creates visual authority. Monospace accents create rhythm. Hierarchy is established through size, weight, and spacing — not color or borders.

3. **Warmth signals approachability** — The warm cream palette and umber accents deliberately avoid the cold, clinical feel of most cybersecurity sites. This positions Alec as someone you'd want on your team, not just someone technically competent.

4. **Motion earns attention** — Animations are minimal and purposeful: section fade-ins, nav transitions, hover states. Nothing loops except the hero scroll indicator. Reduced motion is fully respected.

5. **The motif whispers, never shouts** — The medieval/fantasy influence lives in typography choices (Cormorant Garamond's 16th-century roots), structural language (Arsenal, Dispatches, chapter numbering), and the shield mark. There are zero literal fantasy elements. If a recruiter doesn't notice the motif, the design still works perfectly.
